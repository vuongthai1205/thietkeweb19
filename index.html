Ryū (龍, Dragon)

The Ryu Dragon is a creature of Japanese mythology and folklore that dates back to ancient times. It is an immense serpent-like creature with many long flowing scales and four claws. A Ryu Dragon can grow up to 300 feet long, but is often depicted much smaller. It is most commonly seen as a symbol of power and protection due to its majestic size and strength. In stories and artwork, the Ryu Dragon can be found breathing fire, flying through the air, or even being ridden by a hero. It symbolizes courage, strength, wisdom and loyalty; as well as freedom from danger. Although it represents destruction in some cases, it is more commonly used to convey a sense of awe or admiration for far away lands or creatures not known on Earth. Despite its power, the Ryu Dragon can be tamed by those with the inner courage to do so; this shows how bravery overcomes truly seemingly insurmountable odds. So for viewers throughout history the story of the Ryu Dragon has been an inspirational one depicting how anyone can find success if they have hearts that are brave enough to take on challenges no matter how difficult they may seem.

History

The story of the Japanese dragon Ryu is a tale of courage and chivalry. Ryu was himself born a dragon and fought many battles, often saving those in peril. He was said to be very strong and elegant in battle, displaying great martial prowess. One particular story tells how he was sought after by a powerful emperor who wanted to enslave him. To this end he sent out numerous mages and warriors to find his target; however, every one that came across him were defeated due to Ryu's superior skill in combat. The emperor, being annoyed, placed a price on the dragon’s head which attracted even greater numbers that wished to claim the bounty. Eventually the emperor sent his own daughter to kill the dragon, since none seemed capable of doing so themselves. When she finally tracked down the great beast, what happened next surprised everyone; instead of killing her she offered her help and protection if he would fight alongside her against her father's forces. With their combined strength they managed to defeat the overwhelming army and enlisting help from benevolent gods they managed to turn most of them back into human form thus restoring harmony between man and dragons once more.
